Redlining

What is redlining

In the United States, redlining is an illegal act where people residing in a certain part of a city or town are deemed unworthy of receiving a loan from the bank. This act of redlining is usually due to the race or religion of people living in that particular area. Redlining was put to a stop in 1968 with the fair housing act, though redlining does still happen to minority groups such as black and Latino communities. Compared to white communities, minority communities are at a major disadvantage because of redlining.
